About Apollo

It is a technology company based in Nairobi that sells farm inputs at credit. The farmer chooses the products they need from Apollo's digital store. Picks them up at the agrodealer in their nearest village. The farmer buying on credit can apply for financing and get an instant credit decision, powered by Apollo’s machine learning.Established in 2016, Apollo is a Kenyan technology start-up operating at the intersection of AgriTech and FinTech. Apollo is supplying small Kenyan farmers with high-quality farming inputs on credit, crop insurance and voice-based training. Apollo uses a unique ‘high tech, low touch’ model by leveraging advances in mobile money, machine learning and remote sensing technology including satellite data. All farmers receive agricultural training.

Our Story

Apollo was founded in 2016 by Eli Pollak, Benjamin Njenga, and Earl St Sauver, with the aim of providing farmers with everything they need - from seed to sale - to become successful commercial farmers.

Current Operations

We're growing our team and operations rapidly, to ensure we can service as many farmers as possible. How does it work? Farmers can buy inputs with Apollo in cash or credit. If the farmer is purchasing their items on credit, they apply for financing and get an instant credit decision - powered by Apollo's machine learning credit models. The farmer then chooses the products they need from Apollo’s digital store, and picks them up at the agrodealer in their nearest village. We partner with +1000 agrodealers to ensure maximum proximity to our farmers. All farmers receive best-in-class agricultural training. Farmers who purchase on credit receive insurance to protect their investment from unexpected circumstances. Apollo provides the financing for best-in-market farm inputs. Apollo farmers typically produce 2.5x more than the average Kenyan farmer. At Apollo, our goal is to bring commercial farming to everyone. That means providing farmers the financing they need so they can buy better products, increase their harvest, and turn their subsistence farming into commercial farming. We're redefining investment opportunities for farmers.
